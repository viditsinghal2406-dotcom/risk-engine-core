Learn The System

This section explains how the platform works in simple language. Use the tabs above to explore each topic.

▶

System Flow

Live data flows through feature engineering, a 3-model ensemble scorer, risk classification, and signal generation. Every scored row is persisted and served via a standardised v1 API to downstream systems.

◆

Risk Engine

The system combines Isolation Forest, Z-Score, and LSTM outputs into one score from 0 to 100. Ensemble weights are dynamic and recalibrated after each retrain based on validation performance.

⚙

Decision Layer

Strategy profiles map risk score to BUY, HOLD, or SELL zones with confidence context. Switch between Conservative, Balanced, Aggressive, and Asymmetric on the Decision tab.

△

Signal Integrity

Real signals drive charts and alerts. Synthetic continuity signals are logged separately for observability only and are excluded from performance metrics and backtests.

🔎

Explainability

Every risk score includes per-model reasoning and the dynamic weights used. Available at /api/v1/explain/BTC for full transparency and downstream consumption.

Suggested reading order: Reading Charts -> Risk Levels -> Risk Engine Models -> Decision Layer -> Glossary -> FAQ

ML Ensemble Architecture

The risk score is calculated from 3 independent machine learning models. Weights start at 25% / 25% / 50% and are dynamically recalibrated after each nightly retrain based on each model's validation-set performance.

Isolation Forest (default 25%)

What it does: Detects price and volume patterns that deviate significantly from normal clusters.

How it works: Isolates outliers by randomly building decision trees. Anomalies require fewer splits to isolate, making them easy to identify.

Good for: Detecting complex multivariate anomalies across OHLCV data.

Weakness: May miss gradual trend changes or cyclical patterns.

Z-Score Statistic (default 25%)

What it does: Measures how many standard deviations the current price is from the historical training mean.

How it works: Z = (price - mean) / std_dev. Higher deviation = higher anomaly score.

Good for: Detecting sudden price shocks and extreme values quickly.

Weakness: Assumes normal distribution; may overreact to outliers.

LSTM Neural Network (default 50%)

What it does: Predicts the next price based on a 60-candle sequence. Flags anomalies when actual price diverges from predicted.

How it works: LSTM learns temporal patterns in historical data. Large prediction errors signal unusual market behavior.

Good for: Capturing time-series patterns, trend breaks, and regime shifts.

Weakness: Requires historical data; may lag during sudden market shocks.

Model Consensus

When multiple models agree on an anomaly, confidence increases:

  • 3/3 models agree: HIGH confidence
  • 2/3 models agree: MEDIUM confidence
  • 1/3 models agree: LOW confidence

Trading Signal Logic

Automated buy/sell/hold recommendations based on risk scoring:

Note: The exact risk score thresholds that trigger each signal depend on your chosen strategy. The values below are for the Conservative strategy (default). Switch between Conservative / Balanced / Aggressive / Asymmetric on the Trading Signal tab - the signal guide there always shows the thresholds for your active strategy.

BUY Signal (e.g. Risk = 20 on Conservative)

The market is showing calm, stable conditions with low anomaly detection. This typically indicates:

  • Limited price volatility
  • Predictable market structure
  • Low probability of sudden moves
  • Good risk/reward for position entry

Action: Consider opening or adding to long positions.

HOLD Signal (risk sits between BUY and SELL thresholds)

The market shows mixed signals with moderate anomalies. This typically indicates:

  • Consolidation phase with direction unclear
  • Some volatility but not extreme
  • Need for more data/clarity
  • Potentially ranging market

Action: Maintain current positions; don't add new ones until signal clarifies.

SELL Signal (e.g. Risk = 75 on Conservative)

The market is showing volatile, unstable conditions with frequent anomalies. This typically indicates:

  • High price volatility and unpredictability
  • Multiple model consensus on risk
  • Breakdown of normal patterns
  • Potential for sharp moves

Action: Consider reducing exposure or taking profits. Market risk is elevated.

⚠ These are technical signals, not financial advice. Always conduct your own analysis. Backtesting shows these signals have a ~65-70% directional accuracy rate but past performance does not guarantee future results. Trade at your own risk.

Glossary & Key Terms

Quick reference for all technical terms used throughout this dashboard.

OHLCV
Open, High, Low, Close, Volume - the five values recorded for each price candle. Forms the basis of all chart data.
Risk Score (0-100)
A composite score derived from three ML models. 0 = completely calm market, 100 = extreme instability. Drives all alerts and signals.
Anomaly
A price data point that deviates significantly from expected behaviour, flagged by two or more models. Only High/Critical events (score = 61) are logged.
Isolation Forest
An unsupervised ML algorithm that detects outliers by randomly partitioning data. Anomalies are isolated in fewer steps than normal points. Contributes a dynamic share of the ensemble score (default 25%).
Z-Score
Measures how many standard deviations the current price is from the historical training mean. Formula: Z = (price - mean) ÷ std_dev. Contributes a dynamic share of the ensemble score (default 25%).
LSTM Neural Network
Long Short-Term Memory - a recurrent neural network that learns sequential patterns. Predicts the next price from a 60-candle history window. Contributes a dynamic share of the ensemble score (default 50%).
Ensemble
Combining multiple independent models to produce a more reliable output than any single model. This system uses IF + Z-Score + LSTM with weights that are dynamically recalibrated after each retrain (defaults: 25% / 25% / 50%).
Model Consensus
When 2 or more models independently flag the same point as an anomaly. High consensus = higher confidence in the detection.
Bollinger Bands
Price bands drawn �2 standard deviations from a 20-period moving average. Wide bands signal high volatility; narrow bands signal low volatility.
Moving Average (MA)
The average closing price over the last 20 periods. Smooths out noise to reveal the underlying trend direction. A rising MA = uptrend.
Volatility
Rolling standard deviation of BTC closing prices over a 24-hour window. High volatility means prices are swinging unpredictably - stored in the database with each row.
Contamination Rate
A parameter for the Isolation Forest telling it what fraction of training data to treat as anomalous. Set to 5% (0.05) - a typical conservative value.
Trading Signal
An automated BUY / HOLD / SELL recommendation generated from the current risk score and your selected strategy profile. Not financial advice.
Strategy Profile
A risk-tolerance preset (Conservative, Balanced, Aggressive, Asymmetric) that sets the buy/sell threshold values for signal generation.
Candlestick
A bar on the price chart representing one time period. Green/hollow = price went up; red/filled = price went down. The body shows open?close; the wicks show the high and low.
Support & Resistance
Price levels where the market historically reverses. Support = floor the price bounces from. Resistance = ceiling the price struggles to break through.
Market Cap
Total value of all Bitcoin in circulation = price � circulating supply (~19.8 million BTC). Gives a sense of the overall size of the market.
Liquidity
How easily you can buy or sell without moving the price. High liquidity (high volume) = you can trade large amounts without big price impact.
Bull / Bear Market
Bull = sustained uptrend (prices rising). Bear = sustained downtrend (prices falling). Bitcoin has experienced multiple full cycles historically.
HODL
Crypto slang for "Hold On for Dear Life" - the strategy of buying and holding long-term regardless of short-term price swings, rather than trying to trade in and out.

How to Read the Price Chart

The Chart tab shows BTC's price history using candlesticks - the standard tool used by traders worldwide. Here's everything you need to understand what you're looking at.

What Is a Candlestick?

Each coloured bar on the chart represents one time period (1 hour, 1 day, etc.). It packs four data points into one shape:

Close (top) Open (bottom)

● Green Candle - price went UP

Open (top) Close (bottom)

● Red Candle - price went DOWN

The four parts:

  • Body - thick block, from open to close price
  • Upper wick (shadow) - thin line above body = highest price reached
  • Lower wick (shadow) - thin line below body = lowest price reached
  • Colour - ● green = closed higher than opened; ● red = closed lower

Time Ranges (1H / 1D / 1W / 1M / All)

The buttons at the top of the chart change what each candle represents:

1H1 hour7 daysShort-term intraday moves
1D1 day90 daysWeekly/monthly trends
1W1 week1 yearMajor macro trend cycles

All1 dayAll dataFull history since seeding

ⓘ Tip: You can also scroll/pinch to zoom directly on the chart, and drag to pan. The modebar (top-right on hover) has zoom tools too.

Moving Average (MA 20) - Blue Line

The Moving Average smooths out the price by taking the average of the last 20 candles. It removes noise so you can see the underlying trend more clearly.

  • Price above MA ? trend is upward (bullish)
  • Price below MA ? trend is downward (bearish)
  • Price crossing the MA ? possible trend reversal - watch closely

Bollinger Bands - Yellow Dotted Lines

Bollinger Bands add two bands around the MA: an upper band (MA + 2� standard deviation) and a lower band (MA - 2� standard deviation). About 95% of price action stays inside these bands under normal conditions.

  • Wide bands ? high volatility, the market is moving a lot
  • Narrow bands (squeeze) ? very low volatility, a big move is often coming soon
  • Price touches upper band ? potentially overbought
  • Price touches lower band ? potentially oversold

Toggle MA and Bollinger Bands on/off using the checkboxes in the chart controls.

Anomaly Markers - Circles on Chart

Red and purple open circles overlaid on the chart mark the exact moment a High or Critical risk event was detected. Hover over a circle to see the risk level and score. When you see a cluster of circles, it means the models were repeatedly flagging unusual behaviour around that time period.

  • ● Red circle - High risk (score 61-80)
  • ○ Purple circle - Critical risk (score 81-100)

Volume Bars - Chart Below the Price

The smaller chart below the main price chart shows trading volume - how many USD worth of Bitcoin changed hands during that candle. Volume is one of the most important context clues:

  • Big price move + high volume ? move is significant and likely to continue
  • Big price move + low volume ? move may be weak/false, could reverse
  • Green bar ? buy pressure dominated that period
  • Red bar ? sell pressure dominated that period
Tip: Switch to Line mode if candlesticks feel overwhelming. You will see a cleaner trend path.

? Frequently Asked Questions

Why is the risk score always low / always the same?
The risk score is driven by how unusual today's price looks compared to the last 60 candles the LSTM has learned from. During calm market periods, scores will naturally stay low. The score will spike when something genuinely unusual happens - a sudden crash, a sharp pump, or extreme volume. If you just started the app, it may take a few hours for the model to see enough new data to score confidently.
Why does the Anomaly Rate in Model Stats show 0.0%?
The anomaly rate shown in the Model Registry is the rate at training time - it tells you what fraction of the training data the Isolation Forest deemed anomalous during fitting. For Z-Score and LSTM, the value is always 0.0% because those models don't produce a training-time anomaly rate. This is expected and correct. The Performance tab shows the current live anomaly detection rate, which is more useful.
What's the difference between High risk and Critical risk?
Both signal unusual market behaviour, but Critical (81-100) means all three models are in strong agreement that something extreme is happening. High (61-80) means the ensemble is elevated but not at maximum alarm. Both trigger the anomaly banner and browser notifications, and both are logged in the Anomalies tab.
Can I trust the BUY/SELL signals to trade with?
No - these are algorithmic signals, not financial advice. They are based purely on statistical anomaly detection and risk scoring, not on market fundamentals, news, or expert analysis. They can be wrong. Always do your own research and never invest money you cannot afford to lose.
Why does the 24h % change sometimes look different from what I see on Coinbase or Binance?
The dashboard calculates the 24h change by comparing the current price to the price stored in the database exactly 24 hours ago. Other platforms may use their own specific exchange price or a different reference time. Small differences are normal. Large differences might appear during the first day of use while the database is building up history.
What does "Using Cache" or "Binance" in the status indicator mean?
Live = successfully fetched fresh data from CoinGecko right now. Using Cache = the last fetch was less than 60 seconds ago, so the same data is being shown (to avoid hammering the API). Binance = CoinGecko was unreachable and the system fell back to Binance as a backup. Offline = all live sources failed and the last price stored in the database is being shown.
When do the models retrain?
The models automatically retrain every night at midnight UTC using all available price data in the database. The Isolation Forest and LSTM are re-fitted from scratch so they stay up to date with recent market behaviour. You can see the last retrain time in the Model Stats tab.
Why is volume only showing big numbers (billions)?
Volume is denominated in USD (not BTC units). The 24h USD trading volume for Bitcoin is genuinely in the range of $20-$80 billion on a normal day. This is the standard way volume is reported by CoinGecko and on most professional trading platforms.
How do I change how often the price updates?
Use the Refresh Rate dropdown in the top centre of the header. Options range from 5 seconds to 1 minute. Note: the underlying API has a 60-second cache, so fetching more frequently than once a minute will return the same cached data - it won't hurt anything but also won't give you fresher prices.
What is the Conservative vs Aggressive strategy?
Each strategy sets different thresholds for when to trigger a BUY or SELL signal. Conservative only fires a BUY when the risk score is very low (= 20) and only SELLs at 75+, limiting signals to the very safest/most dangerous moments. Aggressive fires BUY at up to score 35 and SELL earlier, catching more opportunities but also more false signals. Asymmetric is extra cautious on the sell side - good for long-term holders who don't want to exit unless it's truly extreme.
Why a single service instead of separate Flask and FastAPI servers?
The app was refactored to a single FastAPI service that handles both the dashboard (Jinja2 templates) and all API endpoints. This simplifies deployment to Railway: one process, one port ($PORT), one Procfile line. FastAPI is capable of serving HTML templates directly, so there was no reason to run a second Flask process.
What is the v1 API and why does it exist?
The /api/v1/ endpoints (/risk/BTC, /features/BTC, /explain/BTC) output stable Pydantic-validated schemas designed to be consumed by downstream systems in the Series 1 family (1B Regime, 1C Volatility, 1D Contagion, 1E Master Risk, etc.). They are versioned so breaking changes can be introduced in v2 without disrupting consumers.
